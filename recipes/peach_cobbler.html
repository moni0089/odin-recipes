Southern Peach Cobbler

Description

A cobbler is a deep-dish fruit dessert with a sweetened fruit filling that is topped off with a biscuit-like dough. Do not confuse it with crisp or crumble! Peach cobbler can be made with fresh, frozen or canned peaches combined with sugar, spices, lemon juice and cornstarch. The dough is then covered over the dish; it gives you the same fruit-and-pastry combination as a pie, but without all the effort!

Ingredients

  • 8 fresh peaches - peeled, pitted and sliced into thin wedges
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup boiling water
  • Mix together 3 tablespoons white sugar and 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Cooking steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine peaches, 1/4 cup white and brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon juice, cornstarch. Toss to coat evenly, and pour into a 2 quart baking dish. Bake in preheated oven for 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, combine flour, white sugar, brown sugar, baking powder and salt. Blend in butter with your fingertips or a pastry blender, until mixture resembles a course meal. Stir in water until just combined.
  4. Remove peaches from oven and drop spoonfuls of topping over them. Sprinkle entire cobbler with sugar and cinnamon mixture. Bake until topping is golde, about 30 minutes.
